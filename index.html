The Sunday Evening Demo — Qwen3

import torch, forge
from transformers import AutoModelForCausalLM
from peft import get_peft_model, LoraConfig

# 1. Load Qwen model
model = AutoModelForCausalLM.from_pretrained(
    "Qwen/Qwen2.5-0.5B", torch_dtype=torch.bfloat16, device_map="cuda"
)

# 2. Patch with ALL 9 Forge kernels
forge.patch(model)

# 3. Apply LoRA
model = get_peft_model(model, LoraConfig(
    r=16, target_modules=["q_proj", "k_proj", "v_proj",
                          "gate_proj", "up_proj", "down_proj"]
))

Assignment Tips
  • P1 (SwiGLU) and P6 (GeGLU) work on the same kernel file. P1 builds the SiLU path, P6 builds the GELU path. They must agree on the kernel interface at 10:30am. Assign two people who can collaborate closely.
  • P1 (SwiGLU) is the highest-impact kernel (~50% of model compute). Worth thinking carefully about who picks this up, since it sets the pace for downstream integration.
  • P6 (GeGLU) needs strong numerical computing skills — the GELU backward involves erf and exp(-x^2/2), which are trickier than SiLU's sigmoid.
  • P5 (LoRA QKV) is the most complex kernel (GQA asymmetry). Assign someone comfortable with attention mechanics.
  • P7 (Package) is the Day 2 force multiplier. By building the scaffold on Day 1, they eliminate the 2.5-hour serial bottleneck that would otherwise block everyone on Day 2 morning.
  • P8 (Patching + Gemma params + FSDP2) also handles the two trivial Gemma kernel changes (RMSNorm offset=1.0, RoPE base), traces the Gemma module tree, and researches FSDP2. Assign someone who knows HF Transformer internals AND distributed training.
  • Day 2 roles are suggestions — reshuffle based on who finishes Day 1 early vs. who needs help.
